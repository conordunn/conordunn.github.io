Lincoln's diversity

The most and least diverse neighborhoods show wide disparity.

With a population of 3,041 people, Hawley, which sits directly east of City Campus, is the most diverse of all 45 neighborhoods in Lincoln, according to the most recent U.S. Census data. In contrast, Hitching Post Hills in West Lincoln has the least diverse population of 70 people. The chart below (Hitching - blue, Hawley - green) compares the two neighborhoods by ethnicity. In the past 15 years, Hawley's white population has decreased steadily while Asian, Black and Hispanic populations have jumped. On the other hand, Hitching Post Hills' white population has increased, but is seeing very little change in the presence of other ethnicities, the Hispanic population highest there at about 3 percent.

The median household income between the two neighborhoods is also vastly different. The residents in Hitching Post Hills have a median household income of about $81,000. In contrast, the residents in Hawley have a median household income of $32,340, which is a little more than half of the median household income of the average Lincolnite (about $51,000). Eleven percent of Hawley's residents are unemployed versus the 3.6 in Hitching Post Hills. Most of the residents in Hitching Post Hills work some kind of management or administrative-level job, while the majority of the residents in Hawley work in education and construction.

In the neighborhoods themselves, there is great disparity between who owns a home versus who is renting their home. The numbers are almost even between the percentage of residents in Hitching Post Hills who own their home, and the percentage of residents in Hawley who are renting their home. In Hitching Post Hills, the residents own homes on large pieces of land that you have to take dirt roads to get to. It's a very "away from the rest of society" kind of feel. But in Hawley, it's a very different picture. Most of the homes are several years old, many of them have been turned into apartments and the neigborhoods themselves are bordered by businesses on busy streets such as 27th and Vine.
